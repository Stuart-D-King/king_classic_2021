Rules & Format

The highest score you can card is +5 on a hole. Putt everything out and play everything down. All standard rules apply, including the local rule that allows you to drop and take a two-stroke penalty anywhere perpendicular to where a ball is lost or crossed the out of bounds line. Check out this video for a more thorough explanation of the rule.

You must play all six rounds to be eligible to win the King Classic and have your name on the trophy. The winner will have the lowest net score over six rounds.

Initial handicaps must be verified, but do not need to be official. There are several free apps that will calculate a handicap for you. The Grint and Swing by Swing are a few good ones.

This year handicaps will not be adjusted based on performance during the trip. Rather, a course handicap will be calculated for each round based on your initial index and the course rating and slope of the tees you elect to play. A course handicap is calculated as (H.I. x (slope / 113)) + (course rating - course par), rounded to the nearest integer. For example, if your index is 8.2 and you play from the Tournament tees on The Oconee course (6,511 yards, 71.4 rating, 134 slope) your course handicap would be (8.2 x (134 / 113)) + (71.4 - 72) = 9.124 = 9. However, if you decided to play the Three/Four tees on The Oconee (5,862 yards, 68.8 rating, 126 slope) your course handicap would be (8.2 x (126 / 113)) + (68.8 - 72) = 5.943 = 6. Each player will select the tees they wish to play from for each course prior to the trip. Mid-trip changes to your selected tees are not allowed. Anyone under 50 cannot play a tee less than 5,800 yards, and anyone over 50 cannot play a tee less than 5,300 yards.

Please send Stuart a screenshot of your handicap and add yourself to the system by July 20th. You can check what your handicap will for each course here.

Side Games

Skins are based on net low score, and are $5 per round and then split among the participants based on the number of skins won. Please note that if your handicap is greater than 18, for the skins game (and only for the skins game) your handicap will be adjusted to 18. Closest to the pin is $2.50 per round, and the hole has been pre-selected and identified above.

Alex is the bank, so settle up with him. The total for skins and closest to the pin for 6 rounds is $45 ($5 x 6 = $30; $2.50 x 6 = $15; $30 + $15 = $45). If you don't already have it, download and install Venmo on your phone to make money exchange quick and easy. If you don't use Venmo, please bring cash and pay Alex before play begins. He will settle up with you in the same manner that you use to buy in (i.e. Venmo or cash). Alex's Venmo handle is @Alex-King.

Teams

The teams game is $10 per round - $60 total for the trip. At the end of each round, all 16 players will draw a number from a hat to see who their partner was for the round. The top three two-man teams with the lowest net combined score will win the cash prizes accordingly:

First place: $80

Second place: $50

Third place: $30

Ties will result in the splitting of the relevant prizes. For example, a two-way tie for first will see those two teams each winning $65 ($80 + $50 = $130 / 2 = $65), with the third place team winning $30.
